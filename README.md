# Social Media Impact on Mental Health

A data analysis project exploring the relationship between **social media usage** (Facebook, Instagram, YouTube) and **mental health indicators** (e.g., restlessness, depression, difficulty concentrating), with a focus on **young adults**.

This repository contains:
- **`app.ipynb`**: the full end-to-end analysis notebook (data cleaning → preparation → EDA → statistical testing → visuals).
- **`smmh.csv`**: the dataset used in the analysis.
- **`Visualizations/`**: exported plots used to communicate findings.
- **Project write-up files**:  
  - `Social Media's Impact on Mental Health - Article.docx`  
  - `Social Media's Impact on Mental Health - PPT.pptx`

---

## Project Overview

### Motivation
Social media has reshaped communication and entertainment, but concerns have increased about its effects on mental health—especially for young adults. This project investigates whether usage of common platforms correlates with negative mental health outcomes.

### Hypothesis
Higher social media usage—particularly among young adults—correlates with worse mental health outcomes such as:
- restlessness
- depressive feelings
- difficulty concentrating

## Methodology (What the Notebook Does)

### Step 1 — Understand the Dataset
- Load the dataset (`smmh.csv`)
- Inspect data types, missing values, and overall structure

### Step 2 — Load & Inspect + Handle Missing Values
- Identify missing values
- Remove rows with missing data (as done in the project write-up)
- Convert categorical values (e.g., Yes/No) into numeric (0/1)

### Step 3 — Data Preparation
Key preparation steps include:
- Cleaning and transforming categorical variables
- Handling outliers (notably in `Age`)
- Creating age groups:
  - **18–34**
  - **35–54**
  - **55+**
- Converting platform usage indicators (Facebook/Instagram/YouTube) into numeric forms

### Step 4 — Analysis & EDA
The EDA focuses on relationships between platform usage and mental health indicators:

#### A) Group Analysis (t-tests)
Compare mental health indicators between:
- platform **users** vs **non-users**

Platforms tested:
- Facebook
- Instagram
- YouTube

#### B) Correlation Analysis
Compute and visualize a correlation matrix between:
- platform usage variables
- mental health indicators

#### C) Age-Grouped Analysis
Repeat/inspect patterns by age bracket, with special attention to young adults (18–34).

## Findings (Summary)

Based on the analysis:
- Social media usage is associated with increased mental health challenges, particularly among **young adults (18–34)**.
- Instagram usage stands out as notably correlated with increased social comparison and higher depression-related indicators.
- Overall results support the hypothesis that heavier social media usage correlates with worse mental health outcomes (with stronger patterns observed in younger users).

---

## Advice for Young Adults (Practical Takeaways)

If you notice negative effects from social media:
- **Limit screen time** intentionally (daily caps help)
- **Be aware of social comparison** (feeds are curated, not reality)
- **Take breaks** and prioritize offline activities
- **Follow positive influences** (mental health / self-care / education)
- **Seek professional help** if symptoms feel persistent or overwhelming
